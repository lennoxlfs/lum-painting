# Google AI Search Playbook for 2026

This document covers what matters for AI Overviews and AI-supported search experiences according to current Google guidance.

## 1. There is no separate "AI Overview hack"

Google's published guidance is clear:
- the same foundational SEO best practices still apply
- there are no additional technical requirements just to appear
- there are no special AI Overview optimizations required beyond solid SEO and good content

Implication for LFS:
Do not build a fake "AIO-only" SEO strategy.
Build strong pages that deserve visibility in Search overall.

## 2. A page must still be indexed and snippet-eligible

To be eligible to appear as a supporting link in AI features:
- the page must be indexed
- the page must be eligible to appear in Google Search with a snippet
- the page must meet the normal technical requirements and policies

Implication for LFS:
- noindex pages are dead for AI support links
- weakly crawlable pages are weak candidates
- if a page cannot perform in normal Search, it will not be a strong AI-search asset

## 3. What does seem to help in AI-supported search

Google repeatedly points back to:
- helpful, reliable, people-first content
- unique, satisfying, non-commodity content
- strong page experience
- important content available in text
- internal links
- high-quality images and videos when relevant
- structured data that matches visible text
- up-to-date Business Profile and Merchant Center information where applicable

Implication for LFS:
The best AI-search pages are:
- specific
- useful
- complete enough to satisfy a real question
- supported by clear visuals where useful
- connected to related pages through internal links

## 4. AI search changes the query mix

Google says people are asking:
- longer questions
- more specific questions
- follow-up questions
- comparison-style questions

Implication for LFS:
Pages and supporting content should handle:
- nuanced service questions
- comparison questions
- "is this right for me?" questions
- process, pricing-range, expectations, safety, and outcome questions
- decision-stage questions, not just homepage keywords

## 5. Optimize for value of visits, not only click volume

Google says clicks from AI search experiences can be higher quality and more engaged.
It specifically recommends looking beyond raw click count and considering indicators like:
- sales
- signups
- engaged audience
- information lookups about the business

Implication for LFS:
Monthly reporting should look at:
- key events
- calls
- form fills
- booked appointments
- engaged sessions
- service-page engagement
- not just raw organic sessions

## 6. What LFS should build for AI-era visibility

For local businesses, build:
- service pages that answer real pre-decision questions
- city/service pages only when they add real local value
- FAQ support content where it is genuinely useful
- process and expectation sections
- proof and trust sections
- strong visual support
- clean business entity signals

## 7. What LFS should not do

Do not:
- stuff pages with obvious AI-generated filler
- mass-produce nearly identical local pages
- chase every possible long-tail query with low-value text
- assume AI Overviews replace the need for conversion-focused pages
- treat AI visibility as more important than business outcomes

## 8. LFS operating rule

If the content is not:
- useful
- clear
- specific
- textually understandable
- visually supported where useful
- easy to navigate
- worthy of trust

then it is not optimized for either classic Search or AI-supported Search.
