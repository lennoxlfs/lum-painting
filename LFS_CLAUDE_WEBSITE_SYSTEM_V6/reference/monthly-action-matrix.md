# Monthly Action Matrix

Use this reference when monthly reports are available and the goal is to keep SEO, local SEO, and conversion performance moving in the right direction.

## Core rule
Do not stop at reporting.
Turn findings into prioritized actions.

## Action matrix structure
For each important finding, capture:
- metric or report signal
- page, page group, or query group affected
- likely cause
- recommended edit type
- priority
- owner
- validation metric for next month

## Priority ladder
### Priority 1 — blockers
Use first when you see:
- indexing issues
- broken canonicals
- major rendering issues
- severe mobile UX problems
- severe speed issues on key pages

Typical actions:
- fix indexability
- fix canonical rules
- fix broken links or blocked resources
- improve above-the-fold loading and layout stability

### Priority 2 — visibility opportunities
Use when pages have:
- decent impressions but weak average position that could improve with stronger relevance
- incomplete service or city targeting
- thin service explanations
- weak internal links to important pages

Typical actions:
- expand service clarity
- improve topic coverage around the exact service intent
- strengthen city/service connections where legitimate
- add helpful internal links from stronger pages

### Priority 3 — CTR opportunities
Use when pages or queries have:
- strong or rising impressions
- weak CTR compared with position
- uncompetitive or vague snippet presentation

Typical actions:
- rewrite title tags
- rewrite meta descriptions
- improve page heading clarity
- strengthen visible trust/value cues near the top of the page

### Priority 4 — engagement opportunities
Use when GA4 shows:
- landing pages getting traffic but weak engagement
- quick exits from key service pages
- weak scroll depth or low interaction with CTAs

Typical actions:
- improve hero clarity
- move trust signals higher
- sharpen CTA copy
- simplify page flow
- tighten section order and spacing

### Priority 5 — conversion opportunities
Use when pages get traffic and engagement but weak leads.

Typical actions:
- strengthen CTA placement
- clarify offer and next step
- reduce form friction
- add proof near CTAs
- add local reassurance and urgency where appropriate

### Priority 6 — freshness opportunities
Use when local trust and entity signals are stale.

Typical actions:
- refresh photos
- refresh review strategy and replies
- update GBP services and business details
- add new proof or testimonials to priority pages

## Query-to-edit rules
### High impressions + low CTR
Likely focus:
- snippet presentation
- title clarity
- value proposition in meta description
- stronger alignment with intent

### Declining impressions + stable indexing
Likely focus:
- content relevance
- service/city coverage gaps
- weaker internal links
- stronger competitors

### Good traffic + weak leads
Likely focus:
- CTA placement
- form friction
- trust proof
- offer clarity
- page-message mismatch

### Good position + weak clicks
Likely focus:
- title/meta improvement
- SERP differentiation
- more compelling top-of-page copy that matches the promise in search

## Monthly output format
At minimum, produce:
1. Executive summary
2. Top changes in metrics
3. Top affected pages/query groups
4. Recommended edits by priority
5. What to change this month
6. What to measure next month
