# Local SEO and Entity Playbook

## 1. What Google uses for local ranking

Google Business Profile guidance says local visibility is mainly based on:
- relevance
- distance
- prominence

LFS must influence the parts it can influence:
- relevance
- prominence
- clarity of business details
- trust
- website support for the business profile

## 2. Business Profile essentials

Keep complete and accurate:
- business name
- primary category
- additional categories when truly relevant
- address or service area
- phone
- hours
- website link
- services
- photos
- review response activity

## 3. Website support for local ranking

The website should reinforce the profile by making clear:
- what the business does
- where the business serves
- how to contact the business
- why the business is trustworthy
- what next action a user should take

## 4. Entity clarity

Use:
- Organization markup on the homepage
- LocalBusiness markup where relevant
- a consistent brand name
- a consistent phone number
- a consistent official website
- visible and crawlable business details

## 5. Local page architecture for LFS

Good structure:
- Homepage
- Major service pages
- About / trust page
- Contact page
- city/service pages only if they can be made useful
- FAQ or support content only if it adds value

Bad structure:
- dozens of thin city pages
- one generic services page trying to rank for everything
- hidden contact information
- weak or inconsistent NAP / business details

## 6. Local trust signals

Use:
- testimonials
- reviews
- certifications
- team / location photos
- case studies or before-after assets where appropriate
- insurance / licensing / years-in-business where relevant
- clear local references where natural

## 7. Monthly local SEO checks

Every month review:
- GBP completeness and freshness
- new reviews and response gaps
- photo freshness
- landing pages for top services
- service-area coverage
- branded vs non-branded query movement
- local conversion flow
